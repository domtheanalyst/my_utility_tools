5 Top Business Management Tools for Nigerian Tailors to Streamline Operations

Posted by Emmanuel Essang
October 13, 2025
On September 25, 2025
1

Business management tools for Nigerian tailors are essential for scaling your brand in a competitive market like ours. In a market like this, efficiency can make or break your reputation. At Famkapparel, we want to see our partners and customers win, and these tools can help you do so. 

From local SaaS platforms tackling our unique challenges to global Enterprise Resource Planning systems (ERPs) offering robust inventory tracking, these five tools help manage tailor’s orders, measurements, and finances. Discover how business management tools for Nigerian tailors can transform your workshop into a thriving enterprise.

Why Business Management Tools Matter for Nigerian Tailors

The life of an average Nigerian tailor is a very busy one. And what’s worse, you have to do almost everything yourself. You’re the head creative, the marketer, the customer officer, the central database, basically everything. Yes, you have some apprentices, but you probably don’t trust them enough to handle certain things. So you prefer to do them yourself. 

Business management tools will not take all these hats away from you immediately. No, they’re not AI magic erasers for your problems. But they will turn these problems into assets for you. 

You can have a clientele database on the cloud at your fingertips. You can track client measurements with business management tools in one place. Isn’t that better than constantly looking for that dog-eared measurement book you’ve been using for years now? You sef talk true o.

Not just that, with these tools, you can track your projects and plan your time according to deadlines. Tailors in Lagos and other parts of Nigeria, these tools can help you end the “my tailor disappointed me” complaints. Why? Because they take care of these administrative and organizational tasks, freeing you up to focus on churning out fantastic designs for your clients. 

Best business tools for tailors in Lagos include digital ones like those listed below. Let’s begin the journey towards turning administrative chaos into smooth ops. 

Stylebitt  

Business management tools for Nigerian tailors like Stylebitt are helping you ditch paper and pen for digital efficiency. 

Founded in 2021 by Nigerian co-founders Ibrahim Gana and Precious Aleaji, this B2B SaaS platform is designed for African fashion businesses. This software focuses on securing customer measurements, tracking orders, and generating professional invoices to ensure timely deliveries and faster payments.

Some of its strongest features include cloud-based customer data storage and customization options, helping tailors avoid lost measurements and improve fitting accuracy.

In terms of adoption, Stylebitt’s business management tools for tailors are growing in popularity. It has been praised for addressing local operational pain points. Essentially, the creators of Stylebitt are asking, “How many measurement books do you really need?” You can download Stylebitt on Google PlayStore or AppStore.

Fitted  

Fitted leverage AI to make custom garment production efficient and scalable. Launched in 2021 by Ibi Cookey in Nigeria, Fitted is a fashion-tech app that connects over 7,000 tailors with customers across 20 countries. This enables remote measurements, order management, and sales tracking using machine learning technology. 

Fitted has three different sides. One side faces individuals looking to buy finished fashion items, one side is a business management tool for third-party tailors, and one side faces tailors who sew for Fitted Fashion. 

Key features of the management tool include customer info storage, business analytics, and collaboration tools for brands and tailors, ideal for handling diaspora orders or event wear like Agbadas. 

Additionally, Fitted has a vetted tailors list. Once a tailor passes their Tailor Aptitude Test (TAT),  they become eligible to serve Fitted’s network of customers. You can make up to 3x your profits when you join this exclusive list.

Pricing details aren’t publicly listed; you have to create an account to have access to that.

Its adoption rate is strong, with rapid network growth since launch, making it a go-to for tech-savvy Nigerian artisans. You can download the tailor management app on Google PlayStore or AppStore. 

eTailor.ng  

Business management tools for Nigerian tailors like eTailor.ng bring affordable digitization to small workshops across the country. As Nigeria’s first dedicated tailor management system (founded around 2020, founder details not publicly disclosed), it streamlines customer management, order tracking, and financials in one intuitive dashboard.

This tailor management system is trusted by over 700 tailors. It currently manages more than 5000 clients across 31 states in Nigeria. They have a user-friendly interface, a customizable solution, scalable features, real-time analytics, and enhanced security for your data.

Features cover measurement logging, inventory for fabrics, and invoicing, reducing manual errors for garment production. Plans are affordable and scalable, starting with a free plan to test out the app. Here are the available plans and descriptions.

eTailor is focused on simplifying usage for non-tech users. Adoption is rising among small-scale tailors in Lagos and beyond, and this app is positioned as a pioneer for local adoption. 

ApparelMagic  

Global business management tools for Nigerian tailors, such as ApparelMagic, offer enterprise-level ERP tailored for fashion scalability. Founded in 1984 by John Murphy in the US, this cloud-based software manages PLM, CRM, inventory, and orders for apparel brands, adaptable for Nigerian tailors handling bulk runs.

Features include design tracking, cost management, supplier integration, and reporting, with mobile access for on-the-go updates. Pricing tiers: Basic ($120/month, 2 users), Professional ($255/month, 3 users), Enterprise ($495/month, 5 users), Ultimate ($1250/month, 10 users); annual discounts available. All pricings stated here are based on yearly payments, not monthly. See more about pricing for ApparelMagic. 

Widely adopted by thousands of apparel companies globally, it’s ideal for growing tailors with international clientele or exporting designs. 

Uphance  

Business management tools for Nigerian tailors like Uphance provide end-to-end solutions for emerging fashion brands navigating local and international markets. Established in 2017 by Venkat Koripalli and David Helgason in the US, Uphance is a comprehensive ERP for apparel, handling inventory, production, sales, and tech packs with e-commerce integration.

Standout features include order fulfillment, supplier portals, and analytics for optimizing fashion workflows. Plans include Essential, Plus, Premium, and Enterprise. For specific pricing information, contact sales to schedule a meeting. 

Uphance has been adopted by hundreds of fashion brands worldwide, and it supports scalability for Nigerian tailors who can afford them.

Key Takeaways

In conclusion, business management tools for Nigerian tailors like these can automate your workflow, boost client satisfaction, and drive revenue in Nigeria’s dynamic fashion scene. These are some of the best business management tools for tailors in Lagos and the rest of Nigeria; they’re definitely worth checking out.

As a principle, it’s always best to choose a tool that slots into your business model and pace. If you’re a small business, choose something that won’t eat deep into your profit margins. 

When your business scales and your needs become heavier, find a better tool or migrate to a bigger plan within the same tool. Ready to digitize? Start exploring these tools and find which one works for you.
